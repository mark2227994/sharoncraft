Private Founder Playbook

Build SharonCraft like a calm business, not a constant emergency.

This page is for Kelvin Mark✓ as a 21-year-old founder who wants peace, profit, and control, not business pressure every day.

The goal is simple: run SharonCraft in a way that still feels human. Fewer moving parts. Clear systems. Better follow-up. Less stress. More money left over.

Core rule

Your job is not to prove how busy you are. Your job is to build a clean system that still works when you are tired.

Non-Negotiables

These rules protect your peace before they grow the business.

If you keep these steady, SharonCraft can grow without turning into pressure every week.

Keep the path simple

Website to WhatsApp to payment to delivery. If a new idea makes that harder, it is probably not helping yet.

Protect your headspace

Set reply hours, batch tasks, and stop treating every notification like an emergency.

Choose profit over noise

Push the products that are easy to shoot, easy to explain, easy to deliver, and easy to repeat.

Daily Operating Rules

Three things only need to happen each day.

1. Sell

Pick one product, one angle, one post, one WhatsApp status, and one follow-up block.

2. Fulfill

Check orders, confirm payments, and move one real customer closer to delivery.

3. Improve

Fix one product, one page, or one system. No endless tweaking beyond that.

What Not To Do

A lot of business stress comes from avoidable habits.

Do not underprice Do not reply all day Do not launch everything Do not chase every trend Do not mix all your money

You do not need to look like a huge company. You need a business that works calmly and pays you well.

Starter Tools

Small physical tools that make SharonCraft easier to run.

Buy the smallest useful things first. The goal is less stress, better photos, faster packing, and cleaner order flow.

Priority 1
  • Phone tripod for stable photos and reels.
  • Small LED or ring light for more reliable lighting.
  • Simple backdrops like cream board, linen cloth, or a woven mat.
  • Packaging basics like bags, boxes, tissue, stickers, and thank-you cards.
  • Storage boxes for ready products, packaging, sold orders, and props.
Priority 2
  • Power bank for content and customer replies on the go.
  • Extension cable or charging corner to keep your setup calm.
  • Order notebook as a simple backup for customer, product, amount, and delivery.
  • Marker and labels so orders do not get mixed up.
Priority 3
  • Mini lightbox if you want cleaner product-only shots.
  • Thermal label printer if order volume grows.
  • Small props box for trays, stands, filler, and styling fabric.
Best buying order
  • Tripod
  • Light
  • Backdrop
  • Packaging materials
  • Storage boxes
  • Power bank
  • Labels and notebook
Daily Tool Stack

Use the simple tools daily and keep the heavy tools in the background.

This is your normal working system when you want SharonCraft to feel calm, clear, and easy to manage.

Every day
  • `marketing.html` for choosing today’s product, angle, captions, and video ideas.
  • `assistant-admin.html` for uploading products, editing details, and simple catalog updates.
  • Notebook for today’s 3 tasks, follow-up names, quick money notes, and content ideas.
  • WhatsApp for closing sales, answering questions, confirming payments, and delivery follow-up.
  • Instagram for posting and learning what gets real interest.
Weekly
  • Main admin only for bigger setup changes or advanced tasks.
  • Google Search Console to check indexing and visibility.
  • Merchant Center to make sure products and feed health stay clean.
Avoid daily
  • Main admin unless necessary.
  • Website redesigning.
  • Too much analytics checking.
  • Changing many systems at once.
Simple routine
  • Open notebook and write 3 tasks.
  • Open the marketing page and push 1 product.
  • Open assistant admin only if a product update is needed.
  • Reply on WhatsApp and close the important conversations.
  • Stop when the important work is done.
Profit Protection

Simple habits that keep money in the business.

These are the small rules that stop you from working hard and still feeling broke.

Know the true cost
  • Count delivery, packaging, airtime, transport, and your time.
  • Use the website price to protect margin, not just attract people.
  • Review your easiest-profit products often.
Separate business money
  • Do not spend directly from sales money like it is personal cash.
  • Leave part of each sale for restock and emergencies.
  • Pay yourself in a controlled way, even if small.
Favor repeatable winners
  • Keep selling what is easiest to explain and easiest to close.
  • Let slower products earn their space or remove them.
  • Repeat what already gets replies instead of always starting over.
Make customers easier to manage
  • Use saved replies for price, delivery, and follow-up.
  • Keep expectations clear so fewer conversations become stressful.
  • Friendly does not mean always available.
Reset Checklist

When business starts feeling heavy, come back to this.

Use this when you feel pressure building and need to return to the basics.

Am I doing too much at once?

If yes, go back to one product, one post, one follow-up block, and one improvement only.

Am I pricing too low?

If the sale looks good but the money feels weak, your pricing or costs need attention.

Am I reacting instead of leading?

Use your systems. Use the marketing desk. Use the assistant admin. Let the tools carry some of the weight.

Do I need rest before strategy?

Sometimes the next smart business move is a clear head, not another urgent task.

Final Reminder

A peaceful business is still a serious business.

You do not need to suffer to prove SharonCraft is real. Build a business that still feels worth owning.
