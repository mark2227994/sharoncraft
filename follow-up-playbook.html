Private Follow-Up Playbook

Reply clearly, follow up calmly, and keep sales moving without pressure.

This page is your private SharonCraft guide for what to say after a customer shows interest. Keep it simple, warm, and direct.

The goal is not to sound perfect. The goal is to move the customer forward clearly: answer, guide, confirm, and close.

Follow-up rule

One clear message is better than many hesitant messages. Keep your replies useful, kind, and easy to act on.

Core Flow

Use the same simple order every time.

This keeps follow-up from becoming stressful or random.

1. Answer fast

Reply while the interest is still warm. Even a short useful reply is better than silence.

2. Make the next step clear

Do not only answer the question. Tell them what to do next: choose, confirm, pay, or ask one more thing.

3. Follow up once more

If they go quiet, send one warm follow-up. Do not chase too hard.

Do This

Small habits that make follow-up easier.

  • Reply within a reasonable time while interest is still warm.
  • Keep the message focused on one clear next step.
  • Use customer name, product, and area when you know them.
  • Sound human, not overly formal.
  • Use saved replies to reduce stress.
Avoid This

Things that make follow-up feel heavy or ineffective.

  • Sending many messages when one clear message would do.
  • Giving too much information at once.
  • Sounding desperate for the sale.
  • Waiting too long, then over-explaining.
  • Arguing with quiet customers. Follow up once, then move on.
Simple Sequence

Use this when you are unsure what to do next.

It keeps customer handling clear without making you feel like you must be online all day.

Day 0

Reply clearly and help them choose or confirm.

Day 1

If they went quiet, send one warm follow-up.

After payment

Send a short payment or preparation confirmation.

After delivery

Ask politely for a review or a photo.

Keep It Calm

Follow-up should feel like guidance, not pressure.

SharonCraft works best when customers feel helped, not pushed. Clear next steps and warm timing are enough.
